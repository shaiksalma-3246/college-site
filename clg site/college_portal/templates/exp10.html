Experiment 10: Multivariate Analysis of Variance (MANOVA)

Aim:

To apply Multivariate Analysis of Variance (MANOVA) to examine the effect of different teaching methods on multiple dependent variables such as Mathematics and Science scores.
Description:

Multivariate Analysis of Variance (MANOVA) is a statistical technique used to test whether the mean vectors of two or more dependent variables differ across groups defined by an independent categorical variable. It is an extension of ANOVA that considers correlations among dependent variables.

In MANOVA, the null hypothesis assumes that the population mean vectors of all groups are equal. If the null hypothesis is rejected, it indicates that at least one group differs significantly in terms of the combined dependent variables.

Hypotheses:

H0: μ1 = μ2 = μ3 = … (All group mean vectors are equal)
H1: At least one group mean vector is different

MANOVA Test Statistics:

1. Wilks’ Lambda (Λ)
Λ = |E| / |E + H|

where:
E = Error (within-group) sum of squares and cross-products matrix
H = Hypothesis (between-group) sum of squares and cross-products matrix

2. Pillai’s Trace (V)
V = trace[ H(H + E)-1 ]

3. Hotelling–Lawley Trace (T)
T = trace( E-1H )

4. Roy’s Greatest Root (Θ)
Θ = max(λi)

These statistics are used to determine whether teaching methods (Online, Offline, Hybrid) have a statistically significant effect on students’ Mathematics and Science performance.
Code:

import pandas as pd
from statsmodels.multivariate.manova import MANOVA

data = {
    "Teaching_Method": ["Online", "Online", "Online",
                        "Offline", "Offline", "Offline",
                        "Hybrid", "Hybrid", "Hybrid"],
    "Math_Score": [70, 72, 68, 80, 82, 78, 75, 77, 74],
    "Science_Score": [65, 67, 64, 85, 88, 83, 78, 80, 76]
}

df = pd.DataFrame(data)

print("Dataset:")
print(df)

manova = MANOVA.from_formula(
    'Math_Score + Science_Score ~ Teaching_Method',
    data=df
)

print("\nMANOVA Results:")
print(manova.mv_test())

Viva Questions:

1. What is MANOVA?
Ans: MANOVA analyzes multiple dependent variables simultaneously.

2. How is MANOVA different from ANOVA?
Ans: MANOVA handles more than one dependent variable.

3. What are dependent variables here?
Ans: Math_Score and Science_Score.

4. What is Wilks’ Lambda?
Ans: A statistic measuring overall group differences.

5. What does Pillai’s Trace indicate?
Ans: The proportion of explained variance.

6. When is MANOVA preferred?
Ans: When dependent variables are correlated.

7. What is the null hypothesis in MANOVA?
Ans: All group mean vectors are equal.

8. What does p-value < 0.05 indicate?
Ans: Significant difference between groups.

9. What are the teaching methods analyzed?
Ans: Online, Offline, and Hybrid.

10. Where is MANOVA used?
Ans: Education research, psychology, and social sciences.
